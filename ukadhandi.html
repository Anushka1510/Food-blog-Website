MODAK- Ganpati Bappa Morya

This cusine is made in all over maharshtra on the ocassion of ganesh chaturthi. Bappa's favourite modak can be fried as well as steamed. here we will se steamed modak.

Ingredients

For the Outer Covering: 1 cup rice flour 1 1/2 cups water A pinch of salt For the Filling: 1 cup grated fresh coconut 1 cup grated jaggery (adjust to taste) 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder A pinch of nutmeg powder (optional) 1 teaspoon ghee (clarified butter) 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, etc.) and raisins (optional)

Instruction

Step 1: Prepare the Outer Covering Dough Boil Water: In a saucepan, bring 1 1/2 cups of water to a boil. Add Salt and Rice Flour: Add a pinch of salt to the boiling water. Reduce the heat to low and gradually add the rice flour while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.Cook the Dough: Stir the mixture until it forms a smooth dough and starts to leave the sides of the pan. Turn off the heat and transfer the dough to a plate. Let it cool slightly. Step 2: Prepare the Filling Prepare the Coconut-Jaggery Mixture: Heat 1 teaspoon of ghee in a pan. Add the grated coconut and grated jaggery to the pan. Cook on medium heat, stirring continuously, until the jaggery melts and blends with the coconut. This will take about 5-7 minutes. Add Flavorings: Add cardamom powder and nutmeg powder (if using) to the coconut-jaggery mixture. Mix well. You can also add chopped nuts and raisins for extra flavor and texture. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down. Step 3: Shape and Fill the Modaks Prepare Modak Mould: Grease a modak mould with ghee or oil to prevent sticking. Shape the Dough: Take a small portion of the dough and flatten it into a disc with your fingers. Alternatively, you can roll out the dough into small circles. Add Filling: Place a spoonful of the coconut-jaggery mixture in the center of the dough disc. Seal the Edges: Carefully gather the edges of the dough disc and pinch them together to seal, forming a cone-shaped dumpling. Repeat: Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling. Step 4: Steam the Modaks Steam: Place the filled modaks in a steamer or a large pot with a steamer basket. Steam them for about 10-12 minutes on medium heat until they are cooked and firm. Cool: Once cooked, remove the modaks from the steamer and let them cool slightly before serving. Step 5: Serve Serve Warm: Modaks are traditionally served warm or at room temperature. Offer to Lord Ganesha: Offer the freshly made modaks to Lord Ganesha as part of the Ganesh Chaturthi festivities or enjoy them as a sweet treat with your family and friends.
